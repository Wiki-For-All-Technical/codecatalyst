Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 19, 2026

Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and handle your data when you use G2Commons. We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring transparency in our operations.

1. Information We Collect

  • Media Metadata: Titles, descriptions, and categories you provide for images.
  • Authentication Data: OAuth tokens from Google and Wikimedia (stored securely in your session). We do not store your passwords.
  • Usage Logs: Temporary server logs including IP addresses, timestamps, and request details for debugging and performance monitoring.

2. How We Use Data

  • To facilitate the transfer of media from Google services to Wikimedia Commons.
  • To ensure technical stability and prevent abuse of the service.
  • To comply with Wikimedia Cloud Services Terms of Use.

3. Data Storage and Retention

  • No Permanent Storage: We do not store copies of your images on our servers. Images are processed directly from Google to Wikimedia.
  • Session Data: Authentication tokens are stored in server-side sessions and are cleared when you log out or the session expires.
  • Public Availability: Once an image is uploaded to Wikimedia Commons, it becomes public under the specified license.

4. Sharing of Information

We do not share your private information with third parties, except for Wikimedia Commons (as part of the upload process) and Google (to fetch your images).

5. Third-Party Services

This app interacts with Google Photos API and Wikimedia API. Their respective privacy policies also apply:

  • Google Privacy Policy
  • Wikimedia Foundation Privacy Policy

6. Contact

For questions regarding this policy, please contact the maintainers via GitHub.
